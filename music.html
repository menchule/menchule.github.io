Music

Music has been a constant thread throughout my life — a discipline that shaped how I listen, collaborate, and build over time. My background as a double bass player runs in parallel with my professional career, reinforcing structure, patience, and collective performance.

Formal training

I completed my professional music education at the Conservatorio Profesional de Música Rodolfo Halffter, where I trained as a double bass player.

Years of classical training grounded me in technical rigor, ensemble discipline, and the long-term value of deliberate practice.

Orchestral experience

During my formative years, I performed with several youth and training orchestras, including the Joven Orquesta de la Comunidad de Madrid (JORCAM).

Playing in large orchestral settings taught me how individual excellence only matters when it serves the collective result.

Jazz & big bands

Alongside my classical training, I have also played in jazz big bands, with a special focus on the Big Band of the Conservatorio Profesional de Música Rodolfo Halffter.

Jazz introduced a different musical logic: groove, flexibility, active listening, and real-time decision making inside a collective structure.

Electric bass explorations

I have also explored the electric bass in small group settings, experimenting with styles outside the classical and orchestral world.

These projects were short-lived, but valuable — spaces for curiosity, stylistic contrast, and learning by doing.

Current involvement

I am currently an active member of the Orquesta de la Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, combining musical practice with my professional life.

Music remains a living practice for me — not a closed chapter, but a space for focus, listening, and shared creation.

Why music still matters

Music has deeply influenced how I approach work and leadership: timing, structure, adaptability, and trust. An orchestra functions without ego, under a shared direction, and with absolute attention to context.

The same principles apply when building products, teams, or communities.
